Blue Zones

Where do people live the longest — and what do they do differently? A first listening session, scaffolded end to end.
The talk
Dan Buettner — How to live to be 100+
TED · ~19 min total. We use only the opening ~4 minutes: from the start until he finishes explaining the Blue Zones idea (before he travels to the individual places). Stop around 4:00.
Open the talk ↗
N° 1 Before you listen — six words Input first

Read these together before any audio. Say each word aloud, then build one quick sentence of your own. You'll meet all six in the clip — listening is easier once the words are already in the room.

01
longevity
noun
A long life — living for many years.
"The talk is about the secrets of longevity."
Goes with: the secret to longevity.
02
lifestyle
noun
The way you live: your habits, food, work and activity.
"A healthy lifestyle matters more than your genes."
03
genes
noun · adj. genetic
The information you inherit from your parents.
"Health is partly in your genes — but mostly in your habits."
Say: "it runs in the family" / "it's in my genes."
04
premise
noun
The basic idea an argument or study starts from.
"The premise is simple: find the best lifestyle, and you find the formula."
05
optimal
adjective
The best possible.
"He's looking for the optimal diet for a long life."
Synonym: ideal, best.
06
diet
noun
Two meanings: (1) what you usually eat — "a plant-based diet"; (2) eating less to lose weight — "she's on a diet."
"Their diet is mostly vegetables, beans and bread."
Faux ami: FR régime (food) → English diet. English regime = a system or government, never food.
N° 2 First listen — the big idea Audio only · no transcript

Close the transcript. Play from the start. Don't try to catch every word — just listen for one thing:

Listen for this What matters more for a long life — your genes, or the way you live?

Set
N° 3 Second listen — the details Audio only · take notes

Play the same section again. This time, listen for the answers to these. They're all in there — short and findable.

  1. According to the Danish Twin Study, roughly what percentage of how long we live is decided by our genes?
  2. So roughly what percentage is decided by our lifestyle?
  3. He mentions popular diets and advice that people have heard of. Name one.
  4. In your own words: what is the premise of Blue Zones?(What's the basic idea the whole study is built on?)
5:00
Set
N° 4 Check — read it back Transcript on

Now open the transcript on the TED page (the Read transcript tab) and find the answers in the text. This is the point of the session: where the ear missed something, the eye catches it. Notice what you understood by reading that you couldn't catch by listening — that gap is normal, and it shrinks with practice.

First listen — the big idea

The way you live (lifestyle) — about 90% of it — far more than genes.

Second listen — details

1. About 10% (genes).

2. About 90% (lifestyle).

3. Any of: the South Beach Diet, the Atkins Diet, the USDA food pyramid, Oprah, Dr Oz.

4. If we can find the best lifestyle for living long, we can build a rough formula for longevity — because habits, not genes, decide most of it.

If she's stuck on the numbers

The 90/10 stat is the one fact to land. If she misses it by ear, let her find it in the transcript rather than telling her — same self-discovery move as the recordings.
