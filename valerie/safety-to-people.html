From safety
to people

The language for saying what you did — and what it makes you good at now.
First — you write
150–200 words. What you were responsible for in safety, and how those strengths carry into HR.
Then — you talk
Walk me through what you wrote. Don't read it aloud — speak from it.

Saying what you were responsible for

The past — a closed chapter. Past simple.
  • I was responsible for + noun / -ing I was responsible for inspecting the cylinders.
  • I was in charge of + noun / -ing I was in charge of the safety checks on site.
  • I dealt with + noun I dealt with strict regulations every day.
  • My role involved + -ing My role involved training new operators.
  • I made sure (that) + clause I made sure that every procedure was followed.
  • I reported to + person I reported to the site safety manager.

Naming the strength

Not the task — the quality behind it.
Qualities
attention to detail · risk awareness · rigour · reliability · problem-solving · decision-making · staying calm under pressure
Working with rules
compliance · a procedure · a standard · a requirement · to follow / apply / enforce a rule · to keep records
Working with people
to train · to advise · to support · to listen · to raise awareness · to build trust · to handle a difficult conversation

Making the bridge

The heart of the task: old skill → new use.
  • This carries over to + noun This attention to detail carries over to HR work.
  • The same applies to + noun The same applies to employee records.
  • It's useful in HR because + clause It's useful in HR because you deal with rules every day.
  • In safety I …, and in HR I will … In safety I protected people physically; in HR I will protect them differently.
  • What I learned there helps me + verb What I learned there helps me stay objective.
  • Both roles require + noun Both roles require discretion and clear communication.

Where you're heading

The open chapter — still going. Present perfect & present continuous.
  • I have been training in … I have been training in human resources since last year.
  • I am moving into … I am moving into a people-focused role.
  • I have learned that … I have learned that many of my strengths carry across.
  • I would like to … I would like to work on wellbeing and safety culture.

Walking me through it

For the spoken part. Signpost, don't recite.
  • I'd like to start with …
  • The main point is that …
  • Let me give you an example.
  • That's linked to what I said about …
  • What I mean by that is …
  • To sum up, …

Watch these

French words in English costumes.
  • a formation → training / a course I did a training course in employment law.
  • to assist a meeting → to attend I attended the safety meeting every Monday.
  • to control the valves → to check / inspect We checked the valves before filling.
  • actually → currently / at the moment I am currently training in HR.
  • my experiences → my experience My experience in safety is useful here. (uncountable)
  • I work there since 2015 → I worked there from 2015 to 2024 Finished job = past simple.
