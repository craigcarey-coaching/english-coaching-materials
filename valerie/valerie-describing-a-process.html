How it's done

Describing a process, step by step — you know the work; today you put it into English.
Write in full sentences — and say each one out loud before you write it. A complete sentence is not slow, and it is not cheating. It is the thing you are learning. Speaking it aloud first is how it stops being a rule on a page and becomes yours.
Block A — get it down
Write the steps of a process you know well, each as one full sentence in the passive. Order doesn't matter yet.
Block B — put it in order
Number the steps into a clean procedure. Add sequencing words so it flows.
Then — talk me through it
Present it aloud, as if training a new colleague. You keep your writing — I just listen and take notes.
A model — how a delivery is checked into a warehouse

First, the lorry is unloaded and the boxes are placed on a pallet. Each box then is scanned, and the contents are checked against the order. If a box is found to be damaged, it is set aside and a note is made. Once everything has been checked, the goods are moved into storage. Finally, the delivery is signed for and the record is updated.

green = passive verbs (the step matters, not who does it) · rust = sequencing words (they carry the order)
The passive — a reminder
the thing + is / are + past participle
We inspect the cylinder → The cylinder is inspected. Use it when the action matters more than who does it — which is most of a procedure. For a standard procedure, present passive (is filled, are checked) is what you want.
Sequencing — the order words
To start
First · To begin · The first step is
To continue
Then · Next · After that · Once (… is done)
Timing & conditions
Before · After · As soon as · If · When
To finish
Finally · Lastly
Passive verbs for a process — reach for these
Checks & inspection
is inspected · is checked · is tested · is examined · is measured · is recorded
Actions on the object
is filled · is emptied · is sealed · is opened · is closed · is connected · is removed · is replaced · is tightened · is cleaned
Decisions & sign-off
is approved · is rejected · is set aside · is labelled · is signed off · is released
Block A · get every step down
Choose a process you know well — a safety check, an inspection, or how something is prepared, filled or sealed. Write each step as one complete sentence, using the passive. Say each sentence aloud before you write it. Don't worry about the order yet — just get every step down. Aim for one clean sentence per step, not notes.
  • First, the   is  .
  • The   is   before it is  .
  • Once the   is  , the   is  .
  • If  , the   is  .
Block B · put it in order
Now number your steps into the right order — a clean procedure someone could follow. Tighten each sentence and add the sequencing words (first, then, once, before, after, finally) so it flows from one step to the next. When it's numbered, read the whole thing aloud once. If a step sounds wrong, that's your ear working — fix it.
Then · talk me through it
Walk me through your procedure, step by step, as if you were explaining it to a new colleague on their first day. You don't need to show me what you wrote — that's yours. Just speak it. Saying your sentences out loud is the whole point of today: it's how they become yours. Use these to signpost as you go:
  • To start with, …
  • The first thing that is done is …
  • Once that's done, … / After that, …
  • Before that, … is done.
  • The reason for this step is …
  • Finally, …
Watch these — the passive slips
  • the valve checked → the valve is checked  (don't drop is / are)
  • the cylinder is inspect → the cylinder is inspected  (use the -ed participle)
  • the tank is fill → the tank is filled
  • the seal is putted → the seal is put in place  (a few are irregular: put, written, done)
