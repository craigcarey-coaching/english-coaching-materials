Listening · Discussion

AI: The Benefits & The Harms

A short listening built around one idea: why the dangers of AI show up fast, while the good it could do — like curing disease — takes years to arrive.

Source · The Oprah Podcast with Dario & Daniela Amodei, founders of Anthropic (May 2026)

Warm-up

Before any English — just talk.

  • How do you use AI in everyday life, if at all? Has it changed in the last year?
  • When you hear about AI in the news, is the story usually positive or negative? Why do you think that is?

Six words to carry in

Look at each word and the situation. Say what you think it means — then tap to check.

Stretch words if they come up easily: a trade-off (a balance where you gain one thing but lose another), a dynamic (the way forces interact in a situation).

Listen twice

1
For the big idea. Don't worry about every word. Just answer: is the speaker more hopeful or more worried? What is his one main point?
2
For detail. Listen again. This time catch: why the harms come faster, and the example of a future benefit he gives.
One word to listen out for: narrative — he uses it to mean the general "story" people believe about AI right now.

The big idea

Which sentence best captures Dario's main point?

The detail

Try to answer out loud first. Then reveal a model answer.

React

  • Dario says the harms of AI are more visible than the benefits because benefits take longer to arrive. Do you agree? Can you think of an example?
  • He says, in effect, "who cares about the narrative — we want to actually help people." Is the public too negative about AI, or right to be careful?
  • Daniela imagines a world where some diseases are simply gone. Does that sound realistic to you, or too optimistic?

Bring it to your world

The richest language usually lives here.

  • Where is AI already used in your field or industry today?
  • What could it realistically do in your field in five years — and what would you genuinely welcome?
  • What is one risk in your field you'd want controlled or regulated, and how?
  • If you had to give one piece of advice to people leading AI companies, on behalf of your profession — what would it be?
Target language to reach for: a trade-off, to deliver the benefits, a breakthrough, to speed up, transformative, the narrative.
