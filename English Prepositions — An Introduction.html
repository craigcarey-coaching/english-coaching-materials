Prepositions
An Introduction

The little words that tell English where, when, and how. Learn the picture behind each one, then put them to work.

The Idea

Prepositions draw a picture

English doesn't ask "which word goes here?" — it asks "how do I see this situation?" Once you can see the picture, the right word usually follows.

A preposition shows the relationship between things — in space, in time, or in the way an action happens.

Change the preposition and you change the whole meaning. The words are short, but they carry a lot.

One sentence, three pictures
I'm waiting ___ the bus.
atat the bus stop — you're standing at a point, waiting.
onon the bus — you're already inside, on board.
forwaiting for the bus — the bus is the thing you want to arrive.
