Reading aloud

Landing the content word

English doesn't give every syllable equal weight. The meaning rides on a few strong words; everything around them gets swallowed. Your job isn't to read every word evenly — it's to decide which word has to land.

How it works

  1. 1Read the line aloud, naturally, once.
  2. 2Choose the one or two words that carry the meaning.
  3. 3Read it again — hit those words hard, let the rest go light and quick.
  4. 4Reveal the suggestion and compare. There's rarely only one right answer.
